  In article, I want to share about my paths and my thoughts, why I chose this path as my final path.

  I want to share my education first.
  (HIGh SCHOOL)
  In high school. I studied in Food and Beverage department. I thought if I had a skill, it would help me get the job easier. It’s true. When I was looking for a part-time, the restaurant they hired me because I had some related and fundamental knowledge on food. Therefore, they offered me the job, which let me earn my allowance. So, I used to be a waiter, assistant of chef, or bartender. As you know, I have worked in food industry for over 2 years, which let me learn lots of experience. (What I learn from my previous jobs, I write down on another post.) Then, I started to wonder that did I choose chef as my career job. I was 19. I was young. I haven’t finished to travel and explore this world. I still desired to learn a lot, to touch something that I haven’t seen, so I left the restaurant.

  (FJCU University)
  In college, I chose the English department as my major. I considered that English is a global language. If I learn it well, it will help me a lot, on business and future. The beginning of period, it’s hard, because my basics is not well, and Professor is lecturing with English without Chinese. I realize if I don’t spend more time to chase them, I will be left behind. The distance will be longer. When I have progress, they also have progress, so the distance is not easy to be shorter. I have to chase and spend more and more time for filling the gap.

  (Junior in FJCU University)
  When I'm a junior in college, I start to think that what do I want to do in the future? I pretty understand I don’t want to be an English teacher, or a translator. I think it’s not suitable for me. If I do, I will live with unhappiness. So, I start to explore and try everything that I’m curious. Accidently, I touch the programming language. I found this is so interesting, so I start to learn such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, start with Front-End language. This also let me understand the programmer is a job I want to work with for my life. I start to search every resource on google for training my programming skills from freecodeCamp to udemy. I understand I'm not major in CS related, so I purchase some lecture such as Data Structure and Algorithm for increasing my knowledge on CS.

  (End of Article)
  At the end, I want to say, even the path is not straight, but if the destination is same, it's ok with walk a little longer, those paths bring me precious and meaningful memories.

Alan Chen, posted on May 1, 2022.
